Dramatis Personae

main characters

    Protagonist(s): hero(s) / heroine(s)
  • someone who either suffers from actions of antagonist(s), or
  • who senses some kind of lack, or
  • who agrees to liquidate misfortune or lack of another person
  • someone who is supplied with magical agent, or helper, and
  • who makes use of it or is served by it
    • only sibling
    • youngest of three siblings
    • step-sibling
    • lazy / "fool"
    • special from birth
    • brother / sister
    • husband / wife
    Antagonist(s): villain(s)
  • someone whose actions cause protagonist(s) to suffer
    • sibling(s)
    • step-parent
    • step-sibling(s)
    • spouse
    • servant(s), clerk(s)
    • robber(s)
    • merchant(s)
    • evil - tsar / tsarina / knyaz / knyagina
    • night spirit(s)
    • forest spirit(s)
    • water spirit(s)
    • witch
    • dragon
    • koshchei (the deathless)
    Dispatcher:
  • someone who sends protagonist(s) away in search or exile
    • tsar / tsarina
    • knyaz / knyagina
    • elder(s)
    • parent(s)
    • protector(s)
    • spouse
    • sibling(s)
    Donor:
  • someone encountered accidentally (in forest, on road), and
  • who provides the [magical] agent that eventually liquidates misfortune or lack, but
  • first tests protagonist(s) before providing agent
    • animal
    • magical animal
    • wise spouse
    • elderly person
    • wise counselor
    • forest spirit
    • witch
    • baba jaga
    Helper(s): magical agent(s)
  • someone who assists, rescues, solves and/or transfigures protagonist(s)
    • wise spouse
    • animal (bear, wolf, pike, falcon)
    • magical animal (firebird)
    • magical servants (sees-far, runs-fast, eats-all)
    • magical object (replenishing sack / tablecolth, animated club)
    • forest spirit
    • water spirit
    • prophetic
    Person Sought-For(s):
  • someone who exists as a goal and often recognizes and rewards protagonist(s) and / or punishes antagonist(s)
    • tsar / tsarina
    • knyaz / knyagina
    • parent(s)
    • sibling(s)
    • spouse
    False Protagonist(s): false hero(s)
  • antiprotagonist(s) or usurper(s)
  • someone who claims to be the protagonist(s), often seeking and reacting like a real protagonist
    • older siblings
    • step-sibling
    • false "friend"
    • military companion

supporting characters

    family members(s):
  • elder(s)
  • parent(s)
  • protector(s)
  • sibling(s)
  • step-sibling(s)
  • spouse
    connector(s): advance the plot
  • one character finds out something from another, and
  • thereby links one function to the next — advancing the plot
  • information is usually announced, overheard or seen
  • informers
  • complainers, slanderers
  • betrayers
    • beggars
    • servants
    • animals
    • magical objects
Dramatis Personae definitions based on those from: "Morphology of the Folktale by Vladimir Propp" (University of Texas Press, Austin, Texas, 1968), pp.71-91
